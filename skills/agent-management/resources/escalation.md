# Escalation Patterns

## When to Escalate

Escalate when you can't make progress within your scope:
- Reality contradicts assumptions you're working from
- The approach is unworkable
- Scope expanded beyond what was approved
- Blocked on decisions above your authority

## Redesign Brief

When the approach is broken, terminate with a Redesign Brief:

```markdown
## Redesign Brief

**Status:** design-problem | scope-problem
**Trigger:** what failed and where

**Evidence:**
- Concrete facts and artifact pointers
- What was tried and what happened

**Falsification:**
- The assumption that failed
- The contradicting observation

**Blast radius:**
- What must change
- What can stay
- What must be replanned
```

## design-problem vs scope-problem

**design-problem:** The approach is wrong. Needs rethinking.
**scope-problem:** The approach is fine but the breakdown is wrong. Needs
replanning.

Get this right — wrong classification wastes cycles.

## Forcing Through Costs More

Early escalation is cheaper than late discovery. A broken foundation produces
rework that compounds with every layer built on top of it.
